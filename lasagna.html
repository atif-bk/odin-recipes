lasagna Recipes

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering pasta sheets with various fillings, sauces, and cheese. There are several variations, but here’s a general guide to making a traditional lasagna recipe:

Lasagna, with its layers of delicate pasta sheets, rich sauce, and melted cheese, is a dish that tells a story as old as time. Its origins are debated, claimed by both the ancient Greeks and the Italians, but the lasagna we know and love today truly blossomed in Italy, particularly in the region of Emilia-Romagna.

It is in the kitchens of this northern Italian region, amidst rolling hills and fertile valleys, that the most iconic lasagna recipes were born, crafted with the region’s famous Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese and ragù alla Bolognese

Ingredients of Lasagna

  • Lasagna noodles – The pasta sheets that form the layers.
  • Ground beef (or a mix of ground meats) – For the meat sauce.
  • Crushed tomatoes – Base for the tomato sauce.
  • Ricotta cheese – For the creamy cheese layer.
  • Mozzarella cheese – Melty cheese for layering and topping.
  • Parmesan cheese – Adds a salty, nutty flavor.
  • Onion – Sautéed to flavor the meat sauce.
  • Garlic – Adds depth to the sauce.

How to make Lasagna step by step!

  1. Prepare the Meat Sauce
  2. Prepare the Ricotta Mixture
  3. Cook the Lasagna Noodles (if not oven-ready)
  4. Assemble the Lasagna
  5. Top with Cheese
  6. Bake the Lasagna
